Introduction

This dashboard is dedicated to data visualization for the ‘Real Estate and Infrastructure Development Site Selection Techniques” project. San Francisco was selected as an example to develop the methodology. The project focuses on developing methodologies that could be used to evaluate real estate and public infrastructure development site selection. The project proposes to use geospatial analysis (GIS & Python) to capture cities’ spatial characteristics, and thus assist developers and city governors for their site selection decision-making process. For the real-estate development section, a map that divides San Francisco into equal hexagons with development potential and suitability index was developed. As for infrastructure site selection, locations that can optimize the efficiency of city infrastructure service provision were identified.

The real estate activeness index is used to identify a place’s development. Development Suitability

The development suitability index reflects the geographical characteristics and urban environment of the location and measures whether the site is suitable for real-estate development. Slope, land use, floodplain, and contamination were evaluated to calculate the final index (use the dropdown to see individual maps).

Infrastructure Locator

This section displays the result of the techniques we developed to identify the area that needs infrastructure development and sites that can efficiently serve multiple communities. We used kernel density analysis to identify community centers. And based on the community service area for each infrastructure (public library, grocery stores, and public health), optimal infrastructure development locations were identified using Python.
